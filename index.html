📐 What Is the Operating Point?

The Operating Point is the unique (q, Pwf) pair where the reservoir inflow rate equals the wellbore outflow rate. It is the intersection of the IPR (reservoir drive → wellbore) and the VLP (wellbore → surface).

At the operating point: Inflow = Outflow and the well is in hydraulic equilibrium.

📏 Key Equations

1. Linear IPR — Undersaturated (P̄r > Pb)

q = J × (P̄r − Pwf)

J = Productivity Index (STB/d/psi). Valid while Pwf ≥ Pb.

2. Vogel IPR — Solution-Gas Drive (P̄r < Pb)

q/qmax = 1 − 0.2(Pwf/P̄r) − 0.8(Pwf/P̄r)²

qmax = AOF (Absolute Open Flow potential). From Vogel, JPT 1968.

3. Composite IPR — Above & Below Pb

For Pwf ≥ Pb: q = J(P̄r − Pwf)
For Pwf < Pb: q = qb + (J·Pb/1.8)[1 − 0.2(Pwf/Pb) − 0.8(Pwf/Pb)²]

qb = J(P̄r − Pb) is the flow rate at bubble point. From Standing, 1971.

4. ESP VLP Shift

Pwf,ESP = Pwf,natural − ΔPpump
ΔPpump = ρ·g·Hpump / 144 (psi)

ESP lowers the effective Pwf required to lift fluid → VLP curve shifts downward → operating point moves right (higher q).

🔄 Effect of Reservoir Pressure Drop

As P̄r declines over field life:

▼ IPR curve shifts inward/downward
▼ qmax (AOF) decreases
▼ Operating point moves left
▼ Production rate decreases
▼ Pwf at operating point may rise or fall depending on VLP slope

⚠ESP sizing must account for future IPR to avoid premature pump underload or overload as reservoir depletes.
💉 Effect of Stimulation (Acid / Frac)

Stimulation improves near-wellbore skin (S → negative):

▲ J increases (higher PI)
▲ IPR curve shifts outward/upward
▲ qmax increases
▲ Operating point moves right
▲ Higher production at same Pwf

✓Post-stimulation IPR can be modeled using Standing's FE (Flow Efficiency) correction: qstim/qdamaged = FE.
⚡ Effect of ESP Artificial Lift

ESP adds pump head to the system:

▼ VLP curve shifts downward
▲ New operating point at higher q
▲ Lower Pwf required to produce same rate
▲ Pwf can be drawn below Pb
▲ VFD allows frequency tuning to track IPR decline

ℹESP operating point must fall within pump Recommended Operating Window (ROW): typically 70–130% of BEP flow.
