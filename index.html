Operating Point — Core Concept

The Operating Point is the unique (q, Pwf) coordinate where reservoir inflow rate equals wellbore outflow rate. It is the intersection of the IPR curve and the VLP curve at the analysis node.

At equilibrium: Inflow = Outflow. Any perturbation to the system (pressure change, pump change, stimulation) shifts one or both curves and relocates the operating point.

System Node (Pump Intake / Mid-Perforations)
SideComponentCurve
InflowReservoir → PerforationsIPR
OutflowNode → Wellhead → SurfaceVLP (natural)
Outflow+Node → ESP → WellheadVLP − ΔPpump
★
Production optimisation = moving the operating point to the right by either improving IPR (stimulation, skin removal) or lowering VLP (artificial lift, larger tubing, VFD tuning).
📏
IPR Correlation Equations
1 · Linear IPR — Undersaturated (Pwf ≥ Pb)
q = J × (P̄r − Pwf)

J = Productivity Index (STB/d/psi)
2 · Vogel IPR — Solution-Gas Drive (P̄r ≤ Pb)
q / qmax = 1 − 0.2(Pwf/P̄r) − 0.8(Pwf/P̄r)²

qmax = AOF at Pwf = 0
3 · Composite IPR — Standing (1971)
Pwf ≥ Pb : q = J(P̄r − Pwf)
Pwf < Pb : q = qb + (J·Pb/1.8)[1 − 0.2(Pwf/Pb) − 0.8(Pwf/Pb)²]

qb = J(P̄r − Pb)
4 · ESP Nodal — VLP with Pump (Takács 2009)
Pwf,ESP = Pwh + γ·D − ΔPpump + ΔPfriction

ΔPpump = Htotal(ft) × γfluid (psi)
Htotal = Hstage × Nstages × (f/60)²
📉
Reservoir Pressure Decline

As P̄r declines over field life, the IPR curve collapses inward: qmax decreases, the curve shifts left, and the operating point moves toward lower rate and higher drawdown fraction.

ESP sizing must target future IPR (typically P̄r at 3–5 yr forecast) to avoid early underload when the pump is oversized relative to depleted IPR.

⚠Match ESP stages to the mid-life IPR, not initial — avoids both early overload and late underload.
💉
Stimulation Effect

Acidizing or hydraulic fracturing reduces near-wellbore skin (S). Skin removal improves the Flow Efficiency (FE) and shifts the IPR curve outward — higher q at any given Pwf.

Jstim = Jdamaged × FE
FE = (P̄r − Pwf) / (P̄r − Pwf − ΔPskin)
✓Post-frac J can increase 2–10× in tight carbonates. Re-evaluate pump sizing after stimulation.
⚡
ESP Artificial Lift Effect

ESP adds pump head (ΔPpump) at the node, effectively subtracting from the required Pwf for a given rate. The VLP curve shifts downward — operating point moves right (higher q).

VFD allows real-time frequency adjustment to track IPR decline: as reservoir depletes, reduce Hz to stay within ROW (70–130% of BEP).

ℹOperating outside ROW causes axial thrust reversal (<70% BEP) or radial overload (>130% BEP).
